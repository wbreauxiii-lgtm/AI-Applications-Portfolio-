 AI in Retail: Class Notes 
 
 I. Definition and Scope of Retail 
 The retail industry is defined by its proximity to the consumer, encompassing businesses where goods or services are sold for day-to-day living. 
 Physical Retail: Grocery store chains, shopping malls, and in-person boutiques. 
 Online Retail: E-commerce platforms and digital marketplaces. 
 Core Commodities: Food, clothing, electronics, books, and household items. 
 II. Solved AI Use Cases (Current Applications) 
 AI has already transformed retail operations, shifting the industry from simple mass marketing toward data-driven decision-making and personalized customer experiences. 
 1. Personalization and Recommendation Engines: 
 • AI analyzes browsing history, purchase history, and consumer preferences to profile shoppers and recommend products. 
 • Sizing accuracy: platforms such as Amazon suggest sizes based on previous purchases. 
 • Engagement tracking: algorithms track how long users interact with content and use that data to serve targeted ads. 
 2. Demand Forecasting and Inventory Optimization: 
 • AI predicts seasonal demand and helps retailers manage stock more accurately. 
 • Automated markdowns: slow-moving items can be discounted automatically to clear shelf space. 
 • Computer vision in stores: retailers can track how long shoppers stop at products and compare that data with sales figures to estimate demand. 
 3. Chatbots and Virtual Assistants: 
 • Customer support is increasingly automated for major retailers. 
 • Capabilities include order tracking, answering FAQs, and locating items in nearby stores for pickup. 
 4. Dynamic Pricing and Promotion Optimization: 
 • Prices can fluctuate in real time based on demand signals. 
 • Similar to airline pricing, increased interest in a product may raise the price. 
 5. Fraud Detection and Loss Prevention: 
 • AI monitors transactions and in-store activity to identify suspicious behavior. 
 • Self-checkout security: computer vision systems can detect theft or unusual checkout patterns. 
 6. Visual Search and Virtual Try-On: 
 • Visual search tools, such as Google Lens, allow customers to find similar items across retailers by using a photo. 
 • Virtual try-on tools use augmented reality so customers can preview glasses, makeup, furniture, and other products before buying. 
 7. Cashierless/Frictionless Checkout: 
 • Examples such as Amazon Go or Amazon Fresh use sensors and computer vision so customers can shop without a traditional checkout line. 
 III. Evolving Use Cases (Future Goals) 
 Several AI applications in retail are still developing and have not yet reached full maturity because of technical, operational, or economic limitations. 
 Fully Autonomous Retail: Stores with minimal or no human staff, where robotics manage stocking, operations, and delivery. 
 Zero-Waste Retail: Near-perfect forecasting that sharply reduces the large amount of perishable inventory currently wasted. 
 True Omni-channel Personalization: 
 • A seamless shopping experience across platforms, where AI could build complete recommendations across multiple brands and stores. 
 AI Machine Customers: Smart devices, such as refrigerators, that detect low supplies and automatically place orders for consumers. 
 IV. Challenges and Ethical Implications 
 The use of AI in retail creates major concerns alongside its benefits. 
 Privacy and Surveillance: Concerns about extensive customer data collection and the use of facial recognition in stores. 
 Algorithmic Bias: AI may produce unfair pricing, recommendations, or targeting based on demographic patterns in the data. 
 Job Displacement: Automation can reduce the need for cashiers, support staff, and some warehouse roles. 
 Loss of Human Interaction: AI systems often lack empathy and may frustrate customers when handling complex or sensitive issues. 
 Fragmented Systems: Many retailers still use disconnected AI systems, limiting broader cross-platform value. 
 Security and AI Fraud: Synthetic identities, fake reviews, and automated bots can manipulate brand reputation and online trust. 
 V. Key Takeaway 
 Artificial intelligence is reshaping retail by improving personalization, pricing, forecasting, customer support, and security. At the same time, it raises important concerns about privacy, fairness, labor, and the loss of human-centered service.
